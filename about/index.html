About CashCalc — India’s Trusted Cash Denomination Calculator

Last updated: 8 February 2026

Our Mission

CashCalc is a free, open-source Indian cash denomination calculator built for anyone who handles cash daily — bank cashiers, shopkeepers, accountants, students, and depositors across India. Our mission is simple: eliminate the errors, friction, and wasted time that come with manual denomination counting.

India runs on cash. Despite the growth of UPI and digital payments, the RBI’s own data shows that 13,054 crore banknotes were in circulation as of March 2024, worth ₹35.15 lakh crore. Every one of those notes is counted by someone — at a bank, a shop, a temple, an event. CashCalc exists to make that counting faster, more accurate, and error-free.

We are not a large corporation. CashCalc is maintained by a small, dedicated team that cares about getting Indian currency information right — sourced from RBI publications, cross-checked against legislative databases, and reviewed by someone who has actually stood behind a bank counter and counted notes for a living.

Our Editorial Process

We take content accuracy seriously because CashCalc deals with financial information — a category that Google and responsible publishers classify as YMYL (Your Money or Your Life), where errors can cause real harm. Here is how every piece of content on CashCalc is produced:

  1. Research: Supraja identifies the topic based on user needs, not keyword volume. Primary sources (RBI circulars, legislative databases, ICAI guidance notes) are gathered first. We do not publish content based on secondary sources alone.
  2. Drafting: Content is drafted with AI writing assistance to accelerate the initial structure. We use AI as a productivity tool, not as a replacement for subject-matter knowledge. The human editorial team shapes every argument, selects every source, and writes every claim.
  3. Fact-checking: Every data point, legal reference, and banking procedure is verified against the primary source. We link to the original source (RBI.org.in, indiacode.nic.in, ICAI.org, incometax.gov.in) so readers can verify independently.
  4. Expert review: Omprakash reviews the content for procedural accuracy, practical relevance, and alignment with current RBI guidelines. He flags anything that does not match real-world banking practice.
  5. Publication & dating: Content is published with a visible “Last updated” date, author byline, reviewer attribution, and numbered source citations ([1], [2], etc.) linking to original sources.
  6. Corrections: If a factual error is reported or discovered, we correct it within 48 hours. All corrections are documented. We believe in being transparent about mistakes.

AI Disclosure

CashCalc uses AI writing tools to assist with content drafting. We disclose this because we believe in transparency. However, AI does not make editorial decisions — topic selection, fact-checking, source verification, and final approval are all performed by our human team. Every article is reviewed by Omprakash before publication.

We follow Google’s guidance that AI-assisted content is acceptable when it serves the reader and is reviewed for accuracy by qualified humans.

Our Values

We will always be:

  • Free: No charges, no registration, no premium tiers. CashCalc is free for everyone.
  • Private: All calculations happen in your browser. We collect zero user data on any server. Your calculation history stays in your device’s localStorage and never leaves it.
  • Accurate: Every factual claim is sourced from official RBI publications, Indian legislation, or ICAI guidance notes — and reviewed by a banking professional before publication.
  • Honest: We disclose our editorial process, AI usage, and monetisation (advertising via Google AdSense). We do not accept paid content or sponsored articles.
  • Updated: We update our content within 30 days of any RBI denomination policy change. Every page shows a “Last updated” date.

How CashCalc Makes Money

CashCalc is funded through advertising (Google AdSense). We display non-intrusive ads to keep the tool free for everyone. Advertising revenue pays for hosting, development, and content production. We do not:

  • Sell user data (we don’t collect any)
  • Accept paid content, sponsored articles, or paid reviews
  • Charge users for any feature of the calculator
  • Gate content behind registration or paywalls
